Our webcam in Ushuaia teleports users directly to the world's southernmost railway station.

At the foot of the high mountains, overlooking the Beagle Strait, lies the world's southernmost city: Ushuaia, the Capital of Tierra del Fuego.

Our Ushuaia live cam broadcasts the landscape drawn by the zigzagging Pipo River, the Macarena Waterfall and the iridescently colored tree forest. The Southern Fuegina Railroad (Tren del Fin del Mundo) begins here.

Ushuaia's history is closely tied to this 14-kilometer railway — the city was a penal colony, and the railroad was constructed by inmates. Now open to tourists since 1994, the route runs from the End of the World Station to the National Park Station. During the journey, tourists will enjoy spectacular views of the Pipo River Valley as well as La Macarena Falls.

Discover the Enchanting Surroundings of Ushuaia

Welcome to Ushuaia, often referred to as the world's southernmost city, located in the breathtaking region of Tierra del Fuego. This destination offers a plethora of natural wonders and unique experiences for the adventurous traveler. Let's explore what you can visit in the captivating areas around Ushuaia.

1. Tierra del Fuego National Park

Tierra del Fuego National Park is a must-see destination when you’re in Ushuaia. This expansive park is renowned for its stunning landscapes of forests, mountains, and coastlines. The park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering activities like hiking, bird watching, and nature photography.

  • Hiking Trails: Explore numerous trails of varying difficulty, each offering spectacular views of pristine lakes and dense forests.
  • Lapataia Bay: Visit the picturesque bay where the infamous Pan-American Highway ends, marking a significant milestone for road travelers.
  • Bird Watching: Spot rare and diverse bird species, including the impressive Andean condor.

2. The Train of the End of the World

Step aboard the historic Train of the End of the World for a unique journey through some of the most beautiful parts of Tierra del Fuego National Park. This quaint steam railway originally served as transport for prisoners, but today it allows tourists to relive a piece of history as it meanders through stunning natural landscapes.

  • Historic Experience: Discover the rich history of the train and its role in the region's development.
  • Scenic Views: Enjoy breathtaking panoramas from the comfort of your seat, with opportunities to photograph the picturesque landscapes.

3. Martial Glacier

For those who love trekking and panoramic vistas, the Martial Glacier offers an exhilarating adventure. Situated just north of Ushuaia, this glacier provides a perfect outdoor experience, combining splendid landscapes with a touch of physical challenge.

  • Trekking Adventure: Trek up to the glacier to witness stunning views of Ushuaia and the Beagle Channel.
  • Skiing in Winter: In winter, the area turns into a snowy paradise, perfect for winter sports enthusiasts.

4. Beagle Channel

The legendary Beagle Channel is a natural passageway rich with history and incredible wildlife. Embark on a boat tour to fully appreciate the mesmerizing beauty and the extensive marine life of the channel.

  • Wildlife Cruises: Spot sea lions, penguins, and a variety of seabirds, all thriving in their natural habitat.
  • Historic Lighthouses: Gaze upon the iconic Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse, commonly known as the "Lighthouse at the End of the World."

5. Laguna Esmeralda

For a tranquil escape, the hike to Laguna Esmeralda is an absolute treat. This emerald-colored lake is tucked away amidst lush landscapes and provides a serene environment for relaxation and reflection.

  • Scenic Hiking: Begin an enchanting trek through lush lenga forests and across peat bogs to reach the lake.
  • Photography Opportunity: Capture the magical hues of the turquoise water and surrounding mountains, perfect for making memories.

6. The Museum at the End of the World

This small but informative museum in Ushuaia offers insights into the area's natural and cultural history. It's a great way to spend an afternoon learning about the indigenous peoples, explorers, and settlers who have shaped the region.

  • Cultural Exhibits: Discover artifacts and stories from the indigenous Yámana and Selk'nam people.
  • Natural History: Learn about the diverse flora and fauna unique to Tierra del Fuego.

From the majestic landscapes of Tierra del Fuego National Park to the fascinating history of the Train of the End of the World, Ushuaia and its surroundings offer an array of unique experiences. Whether you’re seeking adventure, tranquility, or an educational journey, this enchanting region has something to captivate every traveler.
